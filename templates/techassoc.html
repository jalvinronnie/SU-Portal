Crux

Crux is the programming and computing club of BPHC. It’s a go to place for anyone in campus regardless of their branch to come and showcase their love for coding. Crux organises workshops, events, coding contests and summer class throughout the year to improve the coding culture of the campus. A high level of discipline is expected from the members and as a result, the club ends up with a significant amount of highly skilled people in their respective fields. The club strives to improve everyday and learn new things constantly. Being part of Crux is not gonna be an easy ride, but it’s a rewarding experience. Are you interested in competitive coding, web development, app development, game development or machine learning? This is the club for you! This list doesn’t cover your field of interest? The club is always looking to expand it’s horizons and learn something new from what you can bring to the club.

AERO Club

We are all intrigued by flight, by what causes it, and are often elated by seeing the elegance of flight. Be it a 747, or a paper plane thrown in class, there is something about it that just makes us happy. This new club aims to bring flight to every BITSian, and give them a chance to design, from scratch, their own airplanes, quadcopters, co-axial and spherical drones, and basically anything that flies. The club is equipped and will equip you so that you make flight your own, and experience the elation for yourself. The members go through the physics, the math, the design, and the stories that came with it, work with electronics and codes. So branch is definitely no bar, if you can feel the joy of flight, you are welcome. Come, let's fly.

Automation and Robotics Club

The members of the Automation and Robotics Club are devotees. And their religion is robotics. Robotics is a versatile field which is highly interdisciplinary. Even small projects involve coding, mechanical, electronics and design expertise, which means the members always work in teams and all the projects are group projects. The club’s belief lies in being jacks of all trades instead of trying to be limited to one field, which enables anyone to work on individual interests too. Current projects include autonomous applications like chess-playing using AI, autonomous drawing robot and fields like self-navigation and self-balancing. Also, the club proudly boasts of a full scale robotic arm and a self developed micro-quadcopter, developed from scratch.

Economics association

The Economics Association, as the name suggests, is the association for the MSc Economics students at Hyderabad campus. The primary aim of the association is to inculcate and nurture an interest in the economics and finance world among all students on campus, not just those studying economics. Towards this end, various events are held, both as part of ATMOS, as well as in general. Events conducted during ATMOS include Courtroom - the headliner event you'll soon come to know about, and a business quiz conducted in collaboration with the quiz club, among others. In addition to this, there are several guest talks and workshops conducted throughout the year by some of the best names in the field of finance in the country and abroad. Panel discussions are also held on important current affairs, be it Brexit or the 2016 US elections. Moreover, the association also maintains a blog, where articles written by students and guest contributions are posted regularly. In addition to all of this, the association also conceived and organised the flagship edition of its financial conclave, Finnovate, this year on 4th and 5th February. Guest speakers included a former director at the Bank of America, a NASSCOM member, an Indian School of Business professor among others, apart from top writer on Quora, Mr. Balaji Viswanathan.

IEEE

IEEE, Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers, was formed in 1963 and is dedicated to advancing technological innovation and excellence. It has about 430,000 members in about 160 countries. IEEE is one of the largest professional organisations and is highly involved in the technical program development of numerous events including trade events, training workshops, job fairs, and other programs. IEEE-BITS Pilani Hyderabad is a Student Chapter under the IEEE Hyderabad Region. It boasts of a stellar team of students, with the right blend of technical and non-technical core members. It has organised events such as i-Navigate, Ethical Hacking Workshops, Cisco Networking Workshop, and many more.
